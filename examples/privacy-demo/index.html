🔒 Privacy & Camera Consent

Everything you need to know before enabling your webcam for eye tracking

✅

The Short Version

Your webcam video never leaves your device. All processing happens locally in your browser using TensorFlow.js. No images, video frames, or gaze data are sent to any server.
✓ 100% local processing ✓ No data uploaded ✓ No third-party analytics Open source — GPL-3.0

How Eye Tracking Works

WebGazer-TS processes your webcam feed locally to estimate where on the screen you're looking.

Camera Permission

  • The browser will ask for camera permission when webgazer.begin() is called — not before.
  • You can check the current permission state without triggering a prompt: webgazer.checkCameraPermission()
  • Denying permission is handled gracefully — tracking will not start and your page won't crash.
  • You can revoke camera access at any time via your browser's site settings.
  • Calling webgazer.end() stops the camera stream and removes all DOM elements.
HTTPS or localhost is required for camera access. This is a browser security requirement, not specific to this library.
