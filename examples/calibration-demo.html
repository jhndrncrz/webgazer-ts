Face Mesh (Cyan Dots)

The 468 cyan dots show facial landmarks detected by TensorFlow FaceMesh. These dots help WebGazer track your face and extract eye regions for gaze prediction.

Gaze Dot (Red)

The red dot shows where WebGazer predicts you're looking. It appears after calibration and moves based on your eye position.

📋 How to Use This Demo

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Click "Start WebGazer" - Camera will activate and face detection will begin
  2. Position Your Face - Move until the feedback box turns 🟢 GREEN
    • Face should be centered in the video
    • Sit at a comfortable distance (not too close, not too far)
    • Ensure good lighting on your face
  3. Click "Start Calibration" - 9 red dots will appear on screen one by one
  4. Look at Each Dot - Stare directly at each dot for ~2 seconds
    • Keep your head still, move only your eyes
    • Don't look away until dot disappears
    • WebGazer captures your eye appearance at each position
  5. Test Gaze Tracking - After calibration, move your eyes around and watch the red gaze dot follow!

What Happens During Calibration?

Data Collection: For each calibration point, WebGazer captures multiple snapshots of your eye regions (left eye + right eye patches).

Training: The regression model learns the relationship: "When eyes look like THIS → user is looking at position (X, Y)"

Prediction: After calibration, the model can predict where you're looking based on current eye appearance.
